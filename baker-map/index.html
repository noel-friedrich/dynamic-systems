Click (or drag) in the unit square to add points, then run one baker iteration at a time.

Click inside the square to place points.

Since our bitstring representation of the point coordinates is very accurate, we can choose very specific initial conditions that show different patterns after a chosen number of iterations. For example, the default points are chosen such that secret images appear after iteration 5 and 15. Since the map is very sensitive to the initial conditions, these points are chosen very carefully by hiding the positions in the bitstring (digits) itself.

You can input a sequence of images (letters) and have them appear at specific iteration intervals (10 apart in this example). Try inputting your own text to see how it appears over time.
